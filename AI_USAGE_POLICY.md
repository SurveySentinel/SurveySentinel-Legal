## Principles

SurveySentinel uses artificial intelligence and automated analysis to support, not replace, competent human decision-making. AI features must be designed and operated with safety, legality, transparency, security, data protection and proportionate human oversight in mind.

## Product AI

Product outputs may include quality indicators, scene interpretation, capture recommendations and other decision-support information. Such outputs are probabilistic and may be wrong. The remote pilot, operator and survey professional remain responsible for validating outputs and making operational decisions.

## Prohibited reliance

AI must not be the sole control for collision avoidance, aircraft navigation, emergency response, regulatory compliance, protection of life, legal boundary determination or another safety-critical decision unless specifically certified and approved for that purpose.

## Development controls

AI models and datasets must have documented provenance, permitted usage rights, evaluation criteria, known limitations, security review and release approval. Testing should cover representative operating conditions, foreseeable misuse, failure modes and material performance differences across relevant environments.

## Generative AI by personnel

Confidential information, source code, customer data, credentials, unreleased designs and personal data must not be submitted to an unapproved external AI service. AI-generated code or content requires human review for accuracy, security, provenance, licensing and third-party rights.

## Monitoring and change control

Material model, dataset or decision-threshold changes must be versioned, tested and documented. Serious or recurring failures must be investigated and may require rollback, user notification or suspension of the affected feature.

## Transparency

Public product information should accurately describe the role and limitations of AI without making unsubstantiated claims of autonomy, accuracy or regulatory approval.
