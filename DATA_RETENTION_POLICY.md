> **Important:** This document is a commercial working draft and does not constitute legal advice. It must be reviewed by a qualified UK solicitor before it is relied upon for a product launch, customer contract or processing of personal data.

## Purpose

Personal data and business records must be kept only for a defined and defensible period, then securely deleted or anonymised unless a legal hold or statutory obligation requires continued retention.

## Principles

Retention periods must be based on purpose, contract, legal obligations, limitation periods, security needs and customer expectations. Data must not be retained merely because storage is inexpensive.

## Indicative schedule

The following periods are placeholders and must be validated before launch:

| Record category | Indicative period | Trigger / rationale |
|---|---:|---|
| Account and licence records | Contract term plus 6 years | Contract and legal claims |
| Financial and tax records | 6 years plus current period, or as legally required | Accounting and tax obligations |
| Customer support records | 3 years after closure | Service history and dispute handling |
| Security and access logs | 12 months | Detection, investigation and audit |
| Marketing preferences | While active plus suppression record | Compliance with communication preferences |
| Unsuccessful sales enquiries | 24 months | Follow-up and business records |
| Diagnostic telemetry | 90 days unless aggregated or incident-related | Reliability and security |
| Uploaded project data | Customer-selected period or contract term | Service delivery |
| Vulnerability reports | 6 years after closure | Security history and legal claims |

## Local project data

Data stored only on a user's device remains under the user's control. Users are responsible for their own retention, deletion, backup and legal obligations unless SurveySentinel provides a managed service under separate terms.

## Legal holds and deletion

Routine deletion must be suspended where records are relevant to litigation, investigation, audit, insurance, regulatory inquiry or another legal hold. When the hold ends, normal retention rules resume.

## Secure disposal

Deletion methods must be appropriate to the medium and risk. Backups should expire through documented rotation. Where immediate deletion from immutable backups is impracticable, restored data must remain protected and be deleted again before operational use.
