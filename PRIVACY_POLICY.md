## 2. Scope

This notice explains how we handle personal data relating to website visitors, prospective and current customers, account users, support contacts, beta testers and other business contacts. A separate notice or data processing agreement may apply where SurveySentinel processes data solely on a customer's instructions.

## 3. Data we may collect

Depending on the services used, we may collect:

- identity and business contact information;
- account, authentication and licence information;
- purchasing, billing and transaction records;
- support correspondence and feedback;
- device, application, aircraft, controller and diagnostic information;
- IP address, logs, security events and website usage information; and
- survey or project information intentionally uploaded to an online service.

Local-only product data is not received by SurveySentinel unless the user enables a connected feature, submits it for support or otherwise transfers it to us.

## 4. Purposes and lawful bases

We may process personal data to:

| Purpose | Typical lawful basis |

| Provide accounts, licences, software and support | Contract; legitimate interests |
| Process purchases and maintain financial records | Contract; legal obligation |
| Secure services, prevent fraud and investigate incidents | Legitimate interests; legal obligation |
| Improve reliability and usability using approved diagnostics | Legitimate interests; consent where required |
| Send requested service communications | Contract; legitimate interests |
| Send marketing communications | Consent or legitimate interests, subject to applicable rules and opt-out rights |
| Establish, exercise or defend legal claims | Legitimate interests; legal obligation |

The precise lawful basis must be confirmed against the final service design before launch.

## 5. Sharing

We may share data with vetted hosting, payment, communications, analytics, security, professional-adviser and support providers; regulators or law-enforcement bodies where legally required; and a purchaser or successor in a corporate transaction. Providers must be subject to appropriate contractual and security obligations.

## 6. International transfers

Where data is transferred outside the United Kingdom, we will use an applicable adequacy regulation, the UK International Data Transfer Agreement, the UK Addendum to approved standard contractual clauses, or another lawful safeguard, together with any required transfer-risk assessment.

## 7. Retention

We keep personal data only for as long as reasonably necessary for the stated purpose, legal obligations, security, dispute resolution and enforcement. Indicative periods are maintained in `DATA_RETENTION_POLICY.md`; final periods must be confirmed before production processing begins.

## 8. Security

We use proportionate technical and organisational measures intended to protect personal data. No system is completely secure, and users must protect credentials and promptly report suspected compromise.
