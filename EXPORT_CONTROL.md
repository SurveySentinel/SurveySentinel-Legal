## Policy

SurveySentinel products, software, models, technical data, support and related services must be supplied, exported, re-exported, transferred and used in compliance with applicable export-control, sanctions and trade-restriction laws.

## Responsibilities

Before a controlled transfer or supply, the responsible person must assess:

- the product, software, technology and encryption classification;
- the destination, customer, end user and beneficial owner;
- the intended end use, including military, intelligence, surveillance or weapons-related use;
- applicable UK sanctions and trade restrictions;
- whether a licence, authorisation, registration or record is required; and
- restrictions imposed by third-party technology providers.

## Prohibited activity

SurveySentinel must not knowingly supply products or assistance where prohibited by law, where a required authorisation has not been obtained, or where there is a material risk of diversion to a prohibited destination, party or end use.

## User obligations

Users must comply with applicable controls and must not use, transfer or make SurveySentinel available in breach of sanctions, export controls or contractual restrictions. Users must provide accurate end-use and end-user information when reasonably requested.

## Escalation and records

Potentially controlled transactions must be paused and referred to **[export-control contact]**. Classification decisions, screening results, licences and transfer records must be retained in accordance with applicable law and the Data Retention and Disposal Policy.
