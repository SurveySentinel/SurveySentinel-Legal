## Policy

Only approved open-source software may be introduced into SurveySentinel products, services, build systems or distributed artefacts. Approval must consider legal compatibility, security, maintenance, provenance, export implications and the intended distribution model.

## Approval requirements

Each proposed dependency must have:

- a named owner and business purpose;
- an exact package, source and version;
- a verified licence and copyright notice;
- a security and maintenance assessment;
- a record in the software bill of materials; and
- approval before production use or redistribution.

## Restricted licences

Strong-copyleft, network-copyleft, source-available and non-standard licences, including GPL, AGPL, SSPL and licences containing field-of-use restrictions, require explicit written approval. LGPL components require a documented compliance approach. “Public domain” claims must be verified for the relevant jurisdiction and source.

## Prohibited practices

Do not:

- copy code from forums, repositories or AI tools without provenance and licence review;
- remove copyright, attribution or licence notices;
- use abandoned or unmaintained dependencies without an accepted risk decision;
- distribute a component outside its licence permissions; or
- rely solely on automated licence classification.

## Ongoing obligations

Dependencies must be monitored for vulnerabilities, licence changes, abandonment and end-of-life. The third-party register and software bill of materials must be updated for every release.
