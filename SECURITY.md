> **Important:** This document is a commercial working draft and does not constitute legal advice. It must be reviewed by a qualified UK solicitor before it is relied upon for a product launch, customer contract or processing of personal data.

## Supported versions

Security support applies only to versions explicitly identified as supported in release documentation. Pre-release, development and end-of-life versions may not receive fixes.

## Reporting a vulnerability

Do not disclose a suspected vulnerability publicly. Send a report to **[security email]** containing:

- affected product, version and environment;
- clear reproduction steps or proof of concept;
- potential impact;
- relevant logs or screenshots with personal data removed; and
- preferred contact details.

Do not include live credentials, customer data or unlawfully obtained information.

## What to expect

Until a dedicated response service is established, SurveySentinel aims to acknowledge a credible report within five working days and provide periodic updates. These targets are not contractual service levels.

## Safe-harbour expectations

Good-faith research should avoid privacy violations, service disruption, social engineering, physical testing, data destruction, persistence, unauthorised access beyond what is necessary to demonstrate the issue, and public disclosure before coordinated remediation. A formal safe-harbour commitment must be approved by legal counsel before publication.

## Exclusions

Reports about missing best-practice headers, self-XSS, unsupported versions, rate limits without meaningful impact, or automated scans without evidence may receive lower priority.

## Disclosure

Publication timing must be coordinated through `VULNERABILITY_DISCLOSURE.md`. SurveySentinel may involve affected suppliers, customers, regulators or law enforcement where appropriate.
